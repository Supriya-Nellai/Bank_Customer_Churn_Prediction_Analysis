# 📈 Key Insights

## Overall Churn

- Total Customers: **10,000**
- Total Churned Customers: **2,037**
- Overall Churn Rate: **20.37%**

Approximately one out of every five customers has exited the bank.

---

## Geography Analysis

| Geography | Churn Rate |
|-----------|-----------:|
| Germany | 32.44% |
| Spain | 16.67% |
| France | 16.15% |

### Insight

Customers from **Germany** have nearly double the churn rate compared to France and Spain, indicating a potential region-specific retention issue.

---

## Age Group Analysis

| Age Group | Churn Rate |
|-----------|-----------:|
| 50–59 | 56.04% |
| 40–49 | 30.79% |
| 60+ | 27.95% |
| 30–39 | 10.88% |
| 18–29 | 7.56% |

### Insight

Customers aged **50–59** are the most likely to leave the bank, while younger customers (18–29) have the lowest churn rate.

## Number of Products

## Credit Score Band

| Credit Score Band | Churn Rate |
|------------------|-----------:|
| Poor | 23.73% |
| Fair | 21.06% |

### Insight

---

# Overall Business Recommendations

- Prioritize customer retention efforts in Germany.
- Develop targeted engagement strategies for customers aged 50–59.
- Encourage customers to remain active through personalized offers and regular interactions.
- Investigate why customers with 3–4 products experience higher churn.
- Provide tailored retention campaigns for customers with poor credit scores.
- Monitor high-risk customer segments using predictive analytics to reduce future churn.
